How do I find If something is healthy? By:Jaiana Dixon

  1. Home
  2. Finding Healthy Foods

Everyone should be able to read what is on there food because everyone should know what they are eating and what is going into their bodies. If you know what is in your food, you will know if it is a GMO (Genetically Modified organism). This will allow people to be to see if the ingredients that is in the food you want to buy is healthy and has things in it that you could eat if you are on a specific diet ir trying to just stay healthy.

The steps you take to read the label:

  • Start with looking for both the serving size ( the amount people typically eat at one time)
  • Then look for the number of servings in the package
  • Then check out the total calories by finding out how many calories are in a single serving ( cut back on calories if watching your weight/ trying to lose weight)
  • The use the percent daily values to help evaluate how a particular food fits into your daily meal plan ( daily values are average levels of nutrients for a person eating 2000 calories a day)
  • Next find the high and low daily values ( low is 5% or less, and High is 20% or more)
  • Then you find out the amount of saturated fat, adding sugars and sodium ( eating less saturated fat, added sugars and sodium may help reduce your risk for chronic disease)
  • After you do that you check the ingredients List ( Ingredients are listed in descending order by weight, those in the largest amounts are listed first)

This is how you read the label of your food before you buy it so that you will be able to know what is in your food. And also that way you know how much your eating before you eat it and that way if your on a diet you can count how many calories you eat.
