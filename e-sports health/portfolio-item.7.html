General Esports

Problems that arise in Esports and how to solve them

  1. Home
  2. General health in Esports

What the problem is

If you are a videogamer you already know that the more you play, generally the better you get, two of the most common gaming phrases deal with this concept. The phrase noob which means that you are visibly new to a game and ‘scrub’ which means you may have spent some time on a game, but you're still pretty bad. This culture inspired by obvious progression makes itt painstakingly obvious when someone has spent more time. Famous examples being Silver or Good portraits in overwatch, or the john wick skin in fortnite.

Why it's so hard to solve

In esports every little thing matters, mouse pads, mice, mouse sixe, computer specs, headsets, chairs, and etc. One of those things is practice but when you get paid to play and practice something you also play for fun, you get into a vicious cycle of goofing off, having fun, and actually putting the effort in, also known as try-harding. Players gain so much from each second of playing and training that to stop is to surrender a possible edge you could have on the competition.

Resolutions

As of now most teams don’t limit playtime of games, mandate certain menus, or offer avenues of exercise so it’s left up to players to dictate their own schedule. The thing that stops players for opting for health is the very nature of gaming, Esports is typically feast or famine and since non-league players have to pay entry fees just to compete, and are typically only paid through lasting until semifinals,you won't be able to convince them when it’s not the norm. This leaves it up to the those that own the competition to put standards of practicing to preserve players.
