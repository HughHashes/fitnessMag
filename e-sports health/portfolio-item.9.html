Player Burnout

and how bad it's gotten

  1. Home
  2. Player Burnout

With the growing esports presence in America many new players are being drafted from as young as 15 to play in leagues such as Overwatch contenders, halo world championships, or even online ESL tournaments. With the development of nation wide and international leagues, the volume of players vying for the ability to compete has skyrocketed, and so have medical concerns for player burnout.

Recently in the overwatch league one of the candidates for league mvp has publicized that he’s taking time off and traveling back to his country of south korea as a result of practicing so diligently and being played every game. We’ve seen several other players do the same, snillo, pine, gamsu, and more. While most of the players listed are foreigners(not from america) but it also has to do with the immense dedication they have to give to their sport. Dallas fuel player AKM stated in an interview, “ it usually takes me 100 to 120 akm dragon blades to get good at any hero.” For those not used to overwatchleague slang it too akm 3 minutes and 44 seconds to to charge a dragon blade despite it’s passive time of 4 minutes, so it takes him 4 hours and 35 minutes just to get decent at any given hero, not to mention additional practice of playing into a hero that counters hi, or adjusting to a different playstyle/ meta.

Such intense practice for performances can take up to 6 hours per day leaving players who are new to this training regimine severely tired out. The general opinion of esports by the american public is typically that it doesn’t count as a sport, but the immense commitment it takes from players at the highest of levels can be easily compared to others, especially when it has more severe and long lasting conditions.
