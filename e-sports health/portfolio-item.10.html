Esports players suffering from lung collapses

  1. Home
  2. Esports players suffering from lung collapses

“Janus” Jun-hwa is now a main tank player for stage 2 champions, new york excelsior for the new esports league ,overwatch league’. Participating as a player takes up to 6 hours a day of practicing playing the game in a professional team setting. Players in the league are often hunched over string at screens for both their personal time with streaming, and their professional time scrimmaging and playing live streamed games. Janus also played in Overwatch APEX season 2 with the team LuxuryWatch Blue, where he suffered an onstage lung collapse and missed out on his teams match in the finals.

Lukas “gla1ve” Rossander, the former leader of the Copenhagen Wolves’ CSGO team was participating at the Assembly Winter tournament when after a match he had to disqualify himself on a medical basis, He had suffered a lung collapse, the only reason he noticed that that was is condition, was because this was his third time experiencing it. Viewers remarked at his ailment pointing to the clear impact it had on his game performance. Lukas himself remarked,


“I decided to play the game with a lot of pain as I did not wanted to let my team down. It was probably my worst LAN game ever.”


So what is the unifying theme amongst these and several other players suffering lung collapses? Well scientists point to the players leading an unhealthy lifestyle. Most players are honestly quite skinny and don’t exercise much since playing video games is both their passion and profession. Esports professionals are just now picking up on the importance of physical maintenance. Players are encouraged to both cope with rigorous professional schedules, but build in time for physical and mental exercise.
