No Money, No Gym, No Problem: The Beauty of Calisthenic

Calisthenics are the cheapest and most convenient exercise regimens that most people can do. Calisthenics are any exercises that does use any additional weight. These types of exercise are commonly designed for weight loss and body toning. The name comes from the Greek word kallos which means “perfect.” These exercise are usually based on some form of mechanical or repetitive motion. These exercise usually also help to improve a person’s balance. These workouts are used by everybody from athletes to military units.


Many of the Calisthenics exercise are common and you have likely down them before. Some of these exercise are sit-ups, pull-ups, push-ups, chin-ups, front lever, squats, and burpees. One example of a Calisthenic exercise routine would be to do 50 push-ups, followed by 50 sit-up, followed by 25 burpees, with 30 second breaks in between. You could also do 50 squats, then 25 jump squats, then 50 lunges, also with 30 second breaks. You should do exercises such as these 5 days a week for 3 to 6 months for the most effective and noticeable change. However, it should be noted that while doing this you must keep a stable diet with a low amount of carbohydrates and calories. It is also super important to always stay hydrated in any circumstance, because no workout is useful if you die of dehydration while in the middle of doing a chin-up.
