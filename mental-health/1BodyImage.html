Body Image

by Start Bootstrap
  1. Home
  2. Blog Post

Posted on August 24, 2013 at 9:00 PM

Body image, the subjective picture or mental image of one's own body, as defined by Merriam-Webster. Body image is less of the actual physique of one’s body but more of what they see it as when they look in the mirror. It doesn’t have to be what everyone else sees but what one believes about about their appearance. This includes one’s height, weight, shape, ect. High or even obsessive concern of an “ideal” body image is sadly a very common thing these days, especially among teens.

Self Esteem

Self esteem is is one’s confidence in their self worth or abilities. It can sometimes be based on how you think other people view you. A low self-esteem can come from things like comparing yourself whether it be your looks, weight, or body type to others. Having a high self-esteem is considered to be one of the most important factors in our mental According to Psychology Today, Having a high self-esteem is considered to be one of the most important factors in our mental.

Social Media

Social Media Social media, applications that enable users to create and share content or to participate in social networking. Some of the most popular ones are Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, and Snapchat. They have good impacts like giving inspiration, but it also has a side that can get pretty ugly . You’ll hardly find people’s “bad” pictures on instagram because everyone tries to upload their best moments. Not only that, but many public figures edit/ photoshop their pictures before posting to portray this “perfect” image of themselves with the “best” body, hair, skin, etc. Teens see other people’s best moments and edited images compare themselves and and down themselves in thoughts they aren’t good enough.

Eating Disorder

An eating disorder is the illness of irregular eating habits and/or severe concern about one’s weight or shape, 24 million people suffer from an eating disorder. Many eating disorder cases that occur among teens often develop due to a negative body image. According to Neda, common symptoms of eating disorders include: Appears uncomfortable eating around others, Frequent dieting, Extreme concern with body size and shape, Frequent checking in the mirror for perceived flaws in appearance, Extreme mood swings

In conclusion Body image is often times affected by social media, and a negative body image can lead into have low self-esteem. Many people today, mostly teens fall into the hole of depression and unknowingly acquire eating disorders to fit the “ideal” body type. As trends come and go and “ideal” body types are praised while others are shamed.
