Relaxation by Dagmawit Zeleke

  1. Home
  2. Relaxation

Posted on

Relaxation, the state of being calm and free from anxiety and tension. Many people are overwhelmed with stress and don’t know how to manage it and that can hold them back from accomplishing day to day tasks to the best of their ability. The best way to improve this aspect of your mental health is to find a time in your day, about 5-10 minutes consistently every day and do a relaxation technique of your choice.

Techniques

Relaxation techniques focus on deep breathing and relaxation of muscles. Some techniques include:

  • Meditation, where you can find a quiet place and sit comfortably, and breath naturally.
  • Visualization, where with your eyes open, or closed you’d imagine yourself in a calming place
  • Exercise, do a workout you would enjoy
  • Rest, take some time out of your busy day and take a nap.
  • Yoga, this practice combines postures with breathing.

Benefits

Various forms of relaxation can reduce stress and help your overall mental health. Not only do you feel the difference when relaxing right away, but it also has long term benefits. Stress is arguably the biggest health concern of our time and there is a lot we can do to fix it. Benefits include: relaxing your heart, lowering your risk of catching a cold, boosting your memory, lowering stroke risk, lowering your rate of being depressed, and so much more.

Setting

The setting where you chose to relax can have a big impact on its effectiveness. Trying to relax in a loud room with a bunch of commosson most likely won’t work as well as relaxing in a quiet room. The best places to try to relax is in a dim room.
