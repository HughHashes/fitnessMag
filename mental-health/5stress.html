Stress by Dagmawit Zeleke

  1. Home
  2. Blog Post

Posted on August 24, 2013 at 9:00 PM

Stress; a state of mental or emotional strain or tension resulting from adverse or very demanding circumstances. It is very common thing people deal with. On small doses, it isn’t always a bad thing because it helps people perform under pressure to do their best. But when you’re constantly under stress, your mind and body have to pay the price.

How To Manage It

Stress can’t be avoided 100% but we can make some changes to our lifestyle so that our lives are less stressful. We can learn some techniques to manage the stress, so that we can deal with the parts of our stressful lives that just cannot be changed at that time. A very effective way of stress relief is meditation. Meditating can activate the body’s relaxation response, a state of restfulness that is the opposite of the stress response. If this technique is regularly used, it can lower overall, day to day stress levels.

Why It Happens

Stress often occurs when someone is under pressure, whether it be something they have to accomplish, anticipation, internal/external issues, etc. It is your body’s way of responding to any kind of demand or threat. The stress response is your body’s way of protecting you; examples being, helping you focus and alert. But this is the product of stress in small doses, high stress levels can lead to many problems.

Harms of It

Overly stressing has many harms. It can affect many aspects of your physical and mental health even your social life. These are possible harms of stressing :

  • Depression and anxiety
  • Pain of any kind
  • Sleep problems
  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Digestive problems
