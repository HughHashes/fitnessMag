Positive Thinking by Dagmawit Zeleke

  1. Home
  2. Blog Post

Posted on August 24, 2013 at 9:00 PM

Positive thinking is an essential key to good mental health. The average human being has about 65,000 thoughts a day, in which 95% of those thoughts are from the day before. Each thought you have is associated with the mood you’re in whether it be positive or negative.

Ways To Have Positive Thoughts

There are many techniques for manipulating your thoughts for the better. One is to have a plan. You are far more likely to be getting positive thoughts if you make a plan for exactly what you what to get done. Another is to surround yourself with positive people, although this sounds cliche energy is contagious. So if you surround yourself with negative people, their negative energy rubs off on you. Another way is to give thanks. There are many things in life to be thankful for and stopping to acknowledge them will automatically make you feel more positive.

Importance of Positive Thinking

Positive thinking is important because it reduces or eliminates the negative thoughts that cause stress or depression. It can cause a longer lifespan, lower rates of depression, better cardiovascular health, better coping skills during times of stress, and many more benefits. Also, a negative mindset holds you back. Negative thoughts not only change your mood and affect your health but also get in the way of productivity. With a positive mindset, you can achieve almost anything you set out to get and work for. Those who think positively are happier and more content with their lives.
